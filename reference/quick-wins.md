# Quick Wins

Recommended starting points for teams new to healthcare AI. These "simple" use cases offer fast time-to-value with lower risk.

## By Category

| Use Case | Category | Why It's a Quick Win |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Insurance Card Capture | Document Intelligence | Bounded input, structured output, no clinical risk |
| Patient Message Response Drafting | LLM / Generative AI | Human review before send, routine messages only |
| Duplicate Claim Detection | Anomaly Detection | Clear business rules, financial not clinical |
| Appointment Reminder Channel Optimization | Personalization | Low stakes, easy A/B testing |
| Internal Duplicate Patient Detection | Entity Resolution | Single-system scope, clear success metrics |
| Appointment No-Show Prediction | Predictive Analytics | Operational impact, not clinical |
| FAQ Chatbot | Conversational AI | Scripted fallbacks, narrow scope |

## Common Characteristics

Quick-win use cases share these traits:

- **Human in the loop** — Staff review before patient impact
- **Low clinical risk** — Operational or administrative focus
- **Clear success metrics** — Easy to measure improvement
- **Bounded scope** — Well-defined inputs and outputs
- **Existing workflows** — Fits into current processes

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid starting with:

- Anything that directly influences clinical decisions
- Real-time requirements without fallback options
- Multi-system integrations before single-system success
- Use cases requiring FDA clearance
- Workflows without clear human oversight

## Progression Path

1. **Start** with quick wins to build organizational confidence
2. **Learn** about data quality, workflow integration, change management
3. **Expand** to medium complexity with lessons learned
4. **Scale** to complex use cases with mature operations

---

*Use this as a roadmap, not a prescription. Your organization's readiness matters more than technical complexity.*
