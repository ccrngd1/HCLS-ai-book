- **Problem:** The recipe specifies the deceased-patient-resolution pipeline at flow-and-service granularity but is silent on the identity-boundary policy that controls who can invoke each path and what proves the caller is authorized to act on a particular death event, MPI mutation, reversal operation, family-reported-death intake, personal-representative authorization decision, or cross-recipe event. The chapter-wide pattern from 5.1 through 5.9 has converged on a structured identity-boundary specification; Recipe 5.10 inherits the concern with ten concrete attack surfaces, several of which are uniquely sharp here:

  1. **The per-source ingestion Lambda accepts inbound death events under varying authentication models.** LADMF subscription credentials, state-vital-records-feed credentials per jurisdiction (which themselves vary widely across states), payer-death-feed credentials including CMS MBD's mTLS, hospice-agency credentials, family-reported-death intake from the patient-services call center, obituary-feed credentials, provider-reported-death intake through faxed/mailed/HIE-mediated notifications. The Step 1 `ingest_death_event_from_source()` pseudocode normalizes the source-specific record but the architecture does not specify the per-source authentication validation, the producer-signed envelope schema where the source provides cryptographic signing (CMS MBD does, the modernized FHIR-based VRDR feeds will, the LADMF does at the file-level integrity), the per-Lambda execution-role binding pinned to the specific source-credentials secret reference, or the rejection semantics for sources that fail authentication. A misconfigured source-credential rotation (the institution's per-source secret store falls behind the source's actual rotation cadence; the source's signature is rejected during the cutover) silently drops legitimate death events with the consequent population-coverage gap.

  2. **The death-event-matcher Lambda accepts per-event envelopes that carry the per-source provenance and the source-quality classification.** A forged event with attacker-controlled `source_id` claim, attacker-controlled `source_quality_classification` claim (substituting a high-quality classification for what is actually a low-quality source), or attacker-controlled `provenance` envelope (substituting the supporting-evidence reference) silently propagates through the matcher with the wrong tolerance applied. The recipe's per-source matching tolerance is correctly framed as the calibration discipline, but the per-source-classification authentication is the integrity boundary that makes the classification trustworthy at runtime.

  3. **The mpi-update-handler Lambda's transactional write affects every operational system that consumes the MPI.** A forged death event applied to the MPI without verification produces patient-impersonation-grade insurance termination (recipe 5.4), prescription cancellation (the cascade Lambda flags every active prescription for clinical review; the auto-refill loop is canceled), portal-access suspension (the patient's own account is suspended), care-management enrollment removal, billing-episode closure with post-death billing treatment, and downstream propagation to recipes 5.1 / 5.5 / 5.7 / 5.8 / 5.9 / 3.6 / 7.x / 12.x.
