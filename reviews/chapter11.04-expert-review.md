## Overall Assessment

The opening Marisol-with-the-fourteen-page-PDF vignette earns its position. The text-message Monday morning, the lunch-break completion attempt, the eighty-six-checkbox review of systems, the 14% phone battery, the two-line chief complaint, the family-history detail (paternal grandfather at forty-eight) that gets lost because the form doesn't ask the right follow-up, the fifteen-minute appointment that runs over because the form's data was thin enough that Dr. Adekunle re-asks everything, the constellation of features (chest tightness plus exertional pattern plus family history of early heart disease) that an experienced clinical assistant would chase with adaptive follow-ups but the form does not.   - Cross-Cutting Design Points "The pre-visit packet schema is the bot's clinical-team-facing contract" paragraph and "The conversation log is rich PHI and a clinical record" paragraph.
  - The recipe contains an explicit `<!-- TODO (TechWriter): per anticipated S2 (HIGH) ... -->` marker anticipating this finding.

- **Problem:** Same chapter pattern as 11.3 Finding S2 with recipe-distinct conversation-log-as-clinical-record amplification and pediatric-records-retention-extension amplification. The recipe correctly elevates the pre-visit-packet journal as the durable medication-record-equivalent for intake and the conversation log as rich PHI and a clinical record. Despite this elevation, the architecture pattern, the diagram, and the pseudocode treat the pre-visit-packet-journal write and the tool-call-ledger writes as embedding rather than referencing the per-conversation content. Recipe-acute and recipe-distinct because:

  1. **The pre-visit-packet journal is the recipe's load-bearing clinical-record artifact.** Object Lock in compliance mode for the medical-records-retention window aligns with the audit pipeline retention; the pre-visit-packet journal also has medical-records retention obligations that may be longer than the audit-pipeline retention floor (per-state medical-records retention rules vary; some are 7 years, some 10 years, some "until the patient turns 18 plus N years," some indefinite for certain record classes). For pediatric patients the retention floor is materially longer in many states; the recipe's pediatric-and-proxy-completion variation makes this recipe-acute. The architecture should specify the per-state medical-records retention floor reconciliation, the per-pediatric-record retention extension, and the per-record-class retention regime distinct from the audit-archive.

  2. **The packet's HPI free-text, ROS findings, medication-reconciliation deltas, allergy-reconciliation deltas, history updates, and screener responses are clinical-record elements.** Each has its own retention and access-control profile (some history elements are not part of the formal medical record at all; some screener responses have specific access-control rules; some medication-reconciliation deltas are clinical-decision-support events with their own audit profile).

  3. **The screener responses include item-level data with crisis-sensitivity tags.** PHQ-9 item 9 (suicidal ideation), AUDIT-C items (problem alcohol use), domestic-violence-screener items, and substance-use items all have heightened sensitivity. The packet record's screener_records embed item-level responses. The architecture should specify the per-screener-item access-control surface (some institutions restrict access to item-9 responses to behavioral-health staff; some restrict the item-level data while exposing only the score band).

  4. **The patient's natural-language utterances throughout intake are potentially crisis-sensitive.** A patient mentioning intimate-partner violence, child abuse, elder abuse, or substance-use crisis embeds the disclosure in the conversation log and possibly in the structured history finding.
