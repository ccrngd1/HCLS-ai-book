## Overall Assessment

**Verdict: PASS**

This is the tenth and final recipe in Chapter 3, the chapter-closing capstone at the "Complex" tier with the broadest cross-organizational scope of any recipe in the chapter. The writing meets the moment. The opening Tuesday-pediatrician-in-late-October vignette (three kids from the same elementary school with high fever and dry cough, the chart notes that say "viral syndrome, probably the flu that's going around," the four more kids by Friday, the morning huddle's "are we seeing anything weird?", the school nurse calling the local health department about twenty-two percent absenteeism, the regional pediatric hospital seeing six admissions a day, the state lab's eventual sequencing identifying a novel respiratory variant, and the realization that the first case was probably ten or twelve days earlier than anyone realized) is the densest public-health-surveillance framing in any cookbook recipe and is the closest the cookbook has come to the operational reality of how outbreak detection actually works in most of the country. The reframe to the system-engineering version ("a continuously running pipeline that ingests clinical encounters, lab results, syndromic indicators, pharmacy data, school absenteeism, wastewater pathogen concentrations, and wearable-device aggregate signals; classifies each encounter into a syndromic category; aggregates by geography and time; computes baseline expected counts from historical data; and flags geographic-temporal cells where the observed counts exceed the expected counts") is the system reframe most first-time builders need.

The "Healthcare has a specific structural problem that makes this hard" framing (the baseline isn't a single distribution; it's a pile of overlapping seasonal-trend-residual decompositions, one per geography-syndrome-demographic cell, and the cells you most want signal in are exactly the cells where the baseline is hardest to estimate) is the recipe's biggest pedagogical contribution and is the design lesson most published treatments of syndromic surveillance blur. The signal-to-noise framing ("three excess cases against a background of two thousand routine ones") is operationally accurate and lands the alert-fatigue math in the same shape as Recipes 3.6 through 3.9 with the surveillance-specific twist that the false-positive cost is high (a public health investigation is expensive, and a false alarm that goes public erodes credibility) and the false-negative cost is also high (a missed outbreak is an outbreak that grows).

The thirteen-paragraph "the reason this problem lands at the complex end of the chapter" subsection (the base rate is brutal in both directions; seasonal and structural baselines are non-stationary in ways that matter operationally; multiple comparisons explode; care-seeking behavior is the noise floor; diagnostic coding latency is real; the geographic unit problem; privacy constraints are tight and patient-protective; notifiable disease reporting is its own pipeline; coordination across organizations and jurisdictions is the operational reality; the output's audiences vary) is the densest treatment of public-health-surveillance complexity anywhere in the cookbook. The "the privacy office staff is small" framing of the binding constraint that recurs in Recipes 3.6-3.9 lands here as "a state health department's communicable disease unit might have a few dozen epidemiologists for a population of millions," which is the operational reality the entire program design has to respect.

The Vocabulary You Need subsection (syndromic surveillance, NSSP, ESSENCE, BioSense, notifiable conditions, eCR, NEDSS, NORS, MMWR, line list, case definition, scan statistic, CUSUM and EWMA, Farrington algorithm, wastewater surveillance, sentinel surveillance, HAI surveillance, HIE) is unusually substantive on the public-health-specific terminology that distinguishes outbreak detection from generic anomaly detection. #### Finding S1: Cluster Payload PHI Minimization for Surveillance UI Back End and eCR/NEDSS Connector and OpenSearch Cluster Index Not Stated in Pseudocode

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Expert:** Security
- **Location:** Step 8 `build_clusters` (the `cluster` dict construction with `line_list`, `narrative`, `demographic_breakdown`, `geo_visualization`, `lab_context`, `genomic_context`); Architecture Diagram (`AI[Amazon EventBridge | cluster-bus] --> AJ[Surveillance Team UI]`, `AI --> AK[eCR / NEDSS connector]`, `AI --> AL[(OpenSearch | cluster-index)]`, `AI --> AM[NSSP / NORS / NHSN connector]`); Sample cluster candidate in Expected Results.

- **Problem:** Step 8's `build_clusters` constructs each `cluster` carrying full evidence: `line_list_summary` (de-identified summary), `line_list_pointer` (to the PHI-bearing detail), `demographic_breakdown` (with age-group counts, sex distribution, race/ethnicity distribution), `geo_visualization` (with case coordinates and h3 heatmap), `lab_context` (respiratory panels run, panels positive, positives by pathogen, pcr-panel-negative cases), `genomic_context`, `narrative` (multi-sentence LLM-generated text), `multi_source_concordance`, `tier`. The cluster is published via EventBridge to four subscribers: the Surveillance Team UI back end (AppSync/API Gateway), the eCR/NEDSS connector, the OpenSearch cluster-index, and the NSSP/NORS/NHSN connector.

  The sample cluster candidate in Expected Results confirms the shape. The narrative ("A spatiotemporal cluster of 23 fever-respiratory and ILI ED visits across census tracts 36055-001100 through 36055-001400 over the past 7 days. The observed count is 3.6 times the historical baseline... Cases are demographically skewed toward school-aged children (5-17) who account for 12 of 23 cases. The geographic centroid is within 0.4 miles of three elementary schools serving the affected tracts.   Two recipe-3.10-distinctive amplifications: (1) The line-list-summary aggregates may be re-identifying in small geographies. The recipe correctly addresses this in the Suppressed-Cell-Rules framing ("Counts below the suppression threshold (typically 5 or 10, depending on jurisdiction) cannot be published in geographic detail without re-identification risk"), but the cluster-bus payload doesn't enforce suppression at the publication boundary; the full cluster (with line-list summary at census-tract granularity) flows to all four subscribers regardless of cell counts. (2) The eCR/NEDSS connector sends PHI to external public health authorities under HIPAA's public-health exception; the case-detail data must include patient identifiers for line-list integration, but the cluster-summary payload (the geographies, the demographic distribution, the multi-source concordance) does not need to carry PHI to those external systems beyond the case-by-case eCR submissions. Ninth distinct PHI-minimization-inside-the-BAA surface across the cookbook.

The EventBridge cluster-bus carries only `cluster_id`, `tier`, `composite_score`, geography classification (geography-class only, not specific tract IDs), and syndrome classification (syndrome-class only, not specific syndromes); the consuming back ends (UI, eCR/NEDSS connector, OpenSearch cluster-index, NSSP/NORS/NHSN connector) fetch the full cluster by `cluster_id` through an authenticated path with appropriate IAM scope and per-consumer suppression rules.

  Add a one-line note: "For the cluster event bus, the per-cluster event carries only `cluster_id`, `tier`, `composite_score`, geography-class, and syndrome-class; subscribers fetch the full cluster (with line-list summary, demographic breakdown, geo visualization, lab and genomic context, narrative) from the cluster-state DynamoDB table or the OpenSearch cluster-index through an authenticated path scoped to the consumer's role. The per-consumer fetch applies suppression rules at the read boundary: small-cell counts below the jurisdiction's suppression threshold are aggregated up the geography hierarchy before publication; the eCR/NEDSS connector receives only the structured fields it needs for external reporting and not the full investigator-facing narrative."

- **Expert:** Security
- **Location:** Step 1 `ingest_encounter` (S3 raw events, Kinesis); Step 2 `geocode_and_stratify` (DynamoDB GetItem and PutItem on geocode-cache); Step 4 `update_cell_counters` (DynamoDB UpdateItem on cell-state, Timestream WriteRecords); Step 5 `compute_baselines` (Timestream Query, S3 PutObject on baseline-store, DynamoDB UpdateItem on cell-state); Step 8 `build_clusters` (DynamoDB GetItem and PutItem on cluster-state, OpenSearch index, EventBridge); Step 9 `on_investigator_action` (DynamoDB updates to cluster-state, suppression-rule writes); Prerequisites IAM row.

  For a system that informs public-health investigations, eCR/NEDSS notifiable-condition reporting, cross-jurisdictional notifications, and (when warranted) public-health-advisory issuance, blast-radius minimization matters: a compromised role with broad cluster-state-write access could silently corrupt cluster state (mark a cluster as dismissed when it wasn't, link clusters to wrong geographies, suppress an open cluster), which would propagate into wrong investigation decisions and potentially missed outbreaks.

Event-normalizer Lambda role: read from the surveillance-events stream, write canonical events; no read of cluster-state. Geocoding Lambda role: read and write on geocode-cache; read on Aurora PostGIS reference geographies; no access to encounter PHI beyond the address being geocoded. Syndrome-classifier Lambda role: read enriched events, invoke Comprehend Medical and SageMaker syndrome-classifier endpoint, write classifications. Update-cell-counters Lambda role: write on cell-state DynamoDB and Timestream cell-time-series; no read of cluster-state. Baseline-computer Lambda role: read Timestream cell-time-series, write S3 baseline-store and cell-state expected-counts. Detector-bank Lambdas: read cell-state and Timestream, write per-detector scores to EventBridge and OpenSearch detector-results index. Outcome-capture Lambda role: `dynamodb:UpdateItem` on cluster-state for the cluster being captured; `s3:PutObject` on training-labels bucket only; `events:PutEvents` to the cluster-bus only. Surveillance epidemiologist UI roles: read cluster-state by cluster_id scoped by case-assignment; write outcome-event to the cluster-bus only. eCR/NEDSS connector role: read cluster-state by cluster_id where `condition` is in NOTIFIABLE_CONDITIONS only; eCR-required structured fields. NSSP/NORS/NHSN connector role: read aggregated cluster summary at jurisdiction level; no patient-level data. #### Finding A1: Outcome-Event and Cluster-Grouping Idempotency Not Modeled at the Capture Lambdas

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Expert:** Architecture
- **Location:** Step 8 `build_clusters` (cluster-grouping path with `find_existing_open_cluster` and `update_existing_cluster`); Step 9 `on_investigator_action` (consumed via EventBridge from the surveillance-team UI back end); Architecture Diagram (`AN[Investigator adjudication] --> AO[AWS Lambda outcome-capture]`).

- **Problem:** The architecture is event-driven from the score events stream into `build_clusters` (publishing to the cluster-bus), and from the surveillance team UI back end into `on_investigator_action` (consuming from the cluster-bus). EventBridge guarantees at-least-once delivery; Lambda asynchronous invocation also retries on failure. If EventBridge redelivers a score event, `build_clusters` runs twice on the same proto-cluster:

  1. **Searches for existing open cluster** by `(geographies, syndromes)`. If the first run created a cluster, the second run sees the cluster and routes to `update_existing_cluster`. But if the first run is mid-flight at the time of the redelivery, both runs see no existing cluster and create two duplicate clusters for the same geography x syndrome. The code review's Finding 1 documents the related correctness gap in the Python companion (`find_existing_open_cluster` uses `scan` with `Limit=10 + FilterExpression` which silently misses an existing open cluster when the matching item isn't in the first page); this expert-review finding is the broader architectural pattern at the EventBridge-redelivery level.

  2. **Writes to cluster-state DynamoDB and OpenSearch cluster-index**. Two cluster rows for the same scored geography-syndrome get picked up by the surveillance team queue.

  3. **Publishes a `ClusterOpened` event to the cluster-bus**. Two events, four downstream subscriber invocations each (UI back end, eCR/NEDSS connector, OpenSearch cluster-index, NSSP/NORS/NHSN connector).

  Same applies to `on_investigator_action`: redelivered outcome events double-update the cluster state, double-invoke `initiate_external_reporting` (the eCR/NEDSS/NORS/NHSN/NMI external-reporting clock can be started twice with potentially-different start times), double-invoke `notify_neighboring_jurisdictions` (cross-jurisdictional notifications sent twice), double-invoke `initiate_response_coordination` (response-coordination workflow started twice), double-add the suppression rule, and double-write the training label to S3.

  Same recurring trigger-idempotency pattern as Recipes 2.4-2.10 and 3.1-3.9 (fifteenth consecutive recipe). The fix template is the same.

  For Recipe 3.10 specifically the consequence is calibrated to four pathways: (1) the supervised retraining pathway (doubled label rows on rare positive cases distort class balance for the supervised re-ranker, and confirmed outbreaks are the rarest positive class in any chapter-3 recipe); (2) the cluster-management pathway (duplicate clusters for the same geography-syndrome pair fragment investigations, inflate the surveillance team queue, and break the cluster-grouping intent the recipe explicitly relies on); (3) the notifiable-condition reporting pathway (a redelivered confirmed-outbreak outcome event can initiate the NEDSS or NORS reporting clock twice; state public health statutes specify reporting timelines and a doubled clock initiation produces audit-trail ambiguity that complicates statutory-compliance defense); (4) the cross-jurisdictional notification pathway (duplicate notifications sent to neighboring jurisdictions degrade trust in the federation pattern).

  With this finding now flagged across fifteen consecutive recipes, the per-recipe-edit posture is producing diminishing returns. The cookbook editor should treat the trigger-idempotency appendix as the highest-leverage cookbook-wide editorial investment.

- **Fix:** Two-part fix:
  1. Derive a deterministic event key from each event's identifier and use it as a write-once guard in DynamoDB before the cluster-state write, the OpenSearch index, the EventBridge publish, and the external-reporting/cross-jurisdictional-notification side effects:
     ```
     # Score event idempotency in build_clusters.
     event_key = score_record.score_id + "|" + str(reference_date)
     try:
         DynamoDB.PutItem(
             table = "processed-score-events",
             item  = { event_key: event_key, processed_at: NOW() },
             condition = "attribute_not_exists(event_key)"
         )
     except ConditionalCheckFailedException:
         emit_metric("score_event_duplicate_dropped", 1)
         RETURN

     # Investigator-action idempotency in on_investigator_action.
     action_key = action.action_id    # provided by surveillance UI back end
     try:
         DynamoDB.PutItem(
             table = "processed-investigator-actions",
             item  = { action_key: action_key, processed_at: NOW() },
             condition = "attribute_not_exists(action_key)"
         )
     except ConditionalCheckFailedException:
         emit_metric("investigator_action_duplicate_dropped", 1)
         RETURN
     ```
     The processed-events tables have a TTL of 90 days. Apply the pattern to all event-driven side effects.

  2. Add a "Trigger idempotency" bullet to "Why This Isn't Production-Ready" tying the discipline to the recurring chapter pattern. Strongly recommend a cookbook-wide trigger-idempotency appendix.

#### Finding A2: No DLQ or Poison-Message Handling for the Ingest, Normalize, Classify, Detector, Fusion, Cluster-Builder, or Outcome-Capture Lambdas

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Expert:** Architecture
- **Location:** Architecture Diagram (no `OnFailure` destinations on any Lambda); Prerequisites table.

- **Problem:** No Dead Letter Queue or `OnFailure` destination is configured for any Lambda in the pipeline. Specific failure modes that surface here:

  - **Encounter-ingest Lambda fails on a malformed HL7 v2 ADT or FHIR Encounter payload.** A vendor releases a feed-format change, identifier resolution fails because the EMPI cache is stale, geocoding fails because Amazon Location Service is throttled. Lambda's default async retry is two retries over six hours and then drop. The result is silently lost: no canonical event reaches the syndrome-classifier, no detector scores the event, the cluster never enters the surveillance queue. For a system whose entire value proposition is detecting outbreaks the surveillance team doesn't already see, silently-failing ingestion is the silent-failure mode that most undermines the program.

  - **Lab-ingest Lambda fails during result parsing.** A state public health lab updates its HL7 v2 ORU format, the lab-results DynamoDB write throttles, the pathogen-to-syndrome rule lookup returns an unexpected entity. Same retry-then-drop pattern. Lab-positive cluster signals never enter the detection pipeline.

  - **Wastewater-ingest Lambda fails during NWSS API parsing or direct-lab feed processing.** NWSS API rate limit hit during a thundering-herd refresh, direct-lab credential rotation window misses the per-vendor refresh, sample-concentration parsing fails on a new measurement-unit format. The wastewater anomaly detector receives stale data; the multi-source fusion concordance bonus operates on incomplete signals.

  - **Syndrome-classifier Lambda fails during Comprehend Medical or SageMaker endpoint invocation.** Comprehend Medical throttle, SageMaker syndrome-classifier endpoint cold-start timeout, custom syndrome rule lookup throttle. The encounter is misclassified or unclassified; the cell-counter for the relevant syndrome doesn't increment; the detector misses the event.

  - **Baseline-computer Lambda fails during Timestream Query or S3 baseline-store write.** Multi-year history query times out, Timestream Query throttle, S3 PutObject failure. The baseline isn't refreshed for the cell; the next day's detector runs against a stale baseline; sensitivity drifts.

  - **Detector-bank Lambdas fail.** Control-chart computation throttle, Farrington Flexible regression failure, AWS Batch SaTScan-runner failure. The cell isn't scored; the cluster never gets the signal.

  - **Cluster-builder Lambda fails during cluster construction.** DynamoDB read throttle on cluster-state, OpenSearch indexing failure, Bedrock throttling on the per-cluster narrative generation, line-list-service timeout. The cluster is not built; the surveillance queue is missing a cluster that should have been there.

  - **Outcome-capture Lambda fails on a malformed action event.** The surveillance UI emits an unexpected `outcome` value, the `cluster_id` references a cluster that has aged out, the outcome-text exceeds the DynamoDB attribute size limit. The outcome is never recorded; the cluster stays "open_for_review"; the eCR/NEDSS reporting clock is never initiated for confirmed notifiable conditions; the supervised classifier's next retraining run misses the data point.

  Without DLQs, these failures appear only in CloudWatch Logs and require log-trawling to discover. For a system where the pipeline feeds public-health investigations and notifiable-condition reporting clocks and cross-jurisdictional notifications, silently-failing ingestion or scoring is a regulatory-compliance and program-credibility concern in addition to an operational one.

- **Fix:** Add SQS DLQs to the Architecture Diagram for each Lambda in the pipeline (encounter-ingest, lab-ingest, wastewater-ingest, pharmacy-ingest, absenteeism-ingest, case-ingest, event-normalizer, geocoding, syndrome-classifier, update-cell-counters, baseline-computer, control-chart-detectors, regression-detectors, SaTScan-runner via AWS Batch failure routing, multi-source-fusion, cluster-aggregator, cluster-builder, outcome-capture). Add a Prerequisites note: "Configure each Lambda's `OnFailure` destination to a dedicated SQS DLQ. CloudWatch alarms on DLQ depth alert the on-call surveillance-platform-engineering team; for the encounter-ingest, lab-ingest, syndrome-classifier, baseline-computer, cluster-builder, and outcome-capture DLQs specifically, alarm threshold should be 1 because a single dropped event is a missed encounter, a missed lab result, a missed cell-counter increment, a missed baseline refresh, a missed cluster, or a missed eCR/NEDSS reporting-clock initiation. Replay events from DLQ after fixing the root cause; for events older than the cluster-grouping window, escalate to surveillance-program-governance review rather than auto-replay because the timing-of-detection is itself part of the program's safety case." Add a "DLQ and replay" bullet to Why This Isn't Production-Ready.
