## Overall Assessment

This is the second recipe in Chapter 11 (Conversational AI / Virtual Assistants) and the chapter's first transactional bot recipe after the FAQ bot foundation. - **Expert:** Security (PHI-by-association classification, conversation-log-and-tool-call-ledger-and-booking-event-journal lifecycle, patient-rights-request workflow, transactional-action-as-disclosure-event)
- **Location:**
  - Cross-Cutting Design Points: the chapter-pattern conversation-log-as-PHI primitive carries forward from 11.1 but is not explicitly elevated in 11.2's text; the architecture pattern's audit-and-log-and-telemetry stage references "Durable conversation record" and "Operational telemetry" without specifying the per-channel session-token discipline.

  - Step 1 pseudocode `session = conversation_state_table.get_or_create({channel, channel_session_id, auth_context})` references the session bootstrap with auth_context but does not specify the per-channel session-token discipline.
  - Step 6D pseudocode `booking_event_journal.write({...notes: session.search_parameters.reason_for_visit...})` writes the patient's natural-language reason-for-visit into the durable journal record.
  - Step 10A `audit_record = {... turns: [redact_user_phi(turn) for turn in metadata.turns], tool_calls: [redact_sensitive_args(call) for call in tool_calls] ...}` references the redaction passes by name but the redaction taxonomy is not specified.
  - Why-This-Isn't-Production-Ready "Patient-rights workflow for booking conversations" paragraph: "Conversation logs are PHI by association. The audit pipeline holds the durable record. Patients have rights to access their conversation history. The institution has retention obligations that vary by state and by the institutional regulatory floor."

- **Problem:** The recipe correctly inherits the conversation-log-as-PHI-by-association primitive from 11.1 and correctly elevates the patient-rights workflow in production-gaps. Despite this prose elevation, the architecture pattern, the diagram, and the pseudocode treat the recipe-distinct dimensions as a chapter-pattern reference rather than as architectural primitives. Recipe-acute and recipe-distinct because:

  1. **The authenticated-patient-portal-vs-unauthenticated-web-channel session-token discipline is recipe-distinct.** The recipe correctly notes both channels in Step 1 and in the Variations "Authenticated patient-portal embed" extension. The architecture should specify the per-channel session-token policy: the authenticated-portal channel's session conveys an authenticated patient_id (Step 3A "patient is logged into the patient portal; the session conveys an authenticated patient_id; the bot accepts the patient_id as verified and proceeds"), the unauthenticated channel requires the bot to collect identifiers conversationally and call patient_lookup, and the transition between unauthenticated and authenticated mid-conversation (the patient starts unauthenticated, completes identity verification, and the verified_patient_id is then correlated back to the earlier unauthenticated turns) is the recipe-distinct architectural primitive. The architecture should specify the session-token TTL per channel, the cross-channel session-bridging discipline, and the patient-rights workflow correlation between the channel-session-id and the verified patient_id.

  2. **The transactional-action-as-disclosure-event dimension is recipe-distinct.** Every booking, reschedule, and cancel writes to the institution's scheduling system; this is a clinical-administrative event with a richer disclosure-accounting profile than the FAQ bot's information-only interactions. The architecture should specify the per-action disclosure-accounting log entry (booking_proposed, booking_held, booking_confirmed, booking_failed, booking_compensated lifecycle events flow through EventBridge per the recipe; the disclosure-accounting log discipline integrates with this lifecycle). Each tool invocation that writes to the institution's scheduling system, the eligibility verification system, the notification workflow, or any third-party integration is a third-party-disclosure event recorded with vendor identity, content category, retention commitment, and lawful basis.

  3. **The booking-event journal is a separate compliance-record-class with recipe-distinct retention and access-control surface.** The recipe correctly creates the booking-event journal at Step 6D and correctly classifies it as a durable compliance record under Object-Lock-in-compliance-mode. Despite this, the architecture does not specify the access-control surface (the booking-event journal is part of the institution's clinical-administrative record; access is more restricted than the audit pipeline; clinicians and patient-rights handlers may need read-access; analytics consumers should not have direct access), the retention regime (the booking-event journal retention may align with the medical-records retention floor of the encounter rather than with the audit-pipeline retention; per-state floors apply), the integration with the institution's broader patient-rights workflow (when a patient requests their booking history, the booking-event journal is the source of truth, not the conversation log), and the cross-correlation discipline with the institution's scheduling system (the institution's scheduling system also holds a booking record; the booking-event journal is the bot's audit record of the booking action; the two records have different retention regimes and different access-control surfaces).
